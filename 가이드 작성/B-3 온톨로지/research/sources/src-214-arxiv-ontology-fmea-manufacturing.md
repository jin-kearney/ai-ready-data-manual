## Relevant Excerpt

**Abstract / Key Methodology:**
The paper addresses fault cause identification across manufacturing lines using ontology-guided reasoning combined with process-aware FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis) graph learning and LLMs.

**Ontology-Enabled Causal Reasoning:**
The research leverages domain ontologies to structure manufacturing knowledge and enable systematic causal inference. The ontological framework provides semantic relationships between manufacturing processes, failure modes, and their causes, allowing the system to reason through structured knowledge graphs rather than relying solely on unstructured data.

**FMEA Graph Approach:**
Constructs failure mode and effects analysis as graph structures, capturing dependencies and causal relationships within manufacturing processes. Graph-based representation enables more sophisticated pattern recognition compared to traditional FMEA methods.

**LLM Integration:**
LLMs serve as a key component for extracting and reasoning about fault information, improving the system's ability to understand complex failure descriptions and identify non-obvious causal relationships.

**Cross-manufacturing-line applicability:** System applicable across different production environments and equipment types.

**Authors:** Sho Okazaki, Kohei Kaminishi, Takuma Fujiu, Yusheng Wang, Jun Ota

**Relevance to B-3:**
Shows the practical pipeline: manufacturing ontology (with defect, process, equipment, cause entities and relations) → FMEA graph → LLM reasoning → fault cause identification. The ontology is the "data asset" that makes AI reasoning possible.
