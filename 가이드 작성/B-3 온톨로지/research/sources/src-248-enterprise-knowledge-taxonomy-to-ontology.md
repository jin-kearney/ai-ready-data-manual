## Key Excerpts

**Triggers for moving beyond taxonomy to ontology:**

1. **Managing multiple data sources together:** "A combination of taxonomies and ontologies can connect users to both content and data" rather than just content alone.
2. **Complex interrelationships:** "Modeling complex interrelationships (e.g., in product approval or supply chain processes) across multiple domains."
3. **Sophisticated queries:** Moving beyond basic search to "executing complex multi-part search queries" and "searching across datasets, not just searching for content."

**What taxonomies alone CANNOT do:**
- Relationship exploration: examining "explicit relationships between concepts, not just broader, narrower, or related" ones
- Inference capabilities: "Reasoning based on inferences" that ontologies enable
- Knowledge graph creation: building systems "upon which additional knowledge applications can be built"
- Cross-domain integration: linking "multiple taxonomies and other controlled vocabularies" through unified semantic relationships

**Concrete cross-silo example:**
An organization with separate faceted taxonomies for Reports, Employees, and Media files could convert those facets into ontology classes with semantic relationships (e.g., "Employee _created_ File Type"), enabling unified queries across previously siloed content.

**Manufacturing relevance:**
Analogous to: SOP documents, PFMEA worksheets, MES process records, and equipment manuals each with their own local classification — ontology links them into a unified model where "Process Step _produces_ Component _has defect_ Defect _has cause_ Root Cause _requires action_ Corrective Action."
