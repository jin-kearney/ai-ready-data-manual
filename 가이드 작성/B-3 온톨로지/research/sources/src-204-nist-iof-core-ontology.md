## Relevant Excerpt

**Purpose:**
The Industrial Ontologies Foundry (IOF) developed this mid-level ontology to address a critical gap in manufacturing ontology development. The IOF aims to create "a suite of interoperable ontologies that would serve as a foundation for data and information interoperability in all areas of manufacturing."

**Relationship to BFO:**
The IOF Core is built upon the Basic Formal Ontology (BFO) as its top-level foundation. This tiered architecture ensures manufacturing ontologies are developed in a "structured and mutually coherent manner," with the IOF Core functioning as the essential mid-level component connecting BFO to domain-specific applications.

**Key Focus Areas:**
The publication documents the ontology's role as foundational infrastructure. The IOF Core Ontology resides at the top of a suite from an architectural perspective and contains terms found in several operational areas of manufacturing. Initial focus is on support for design, maintenance, supply chain, production, and lifecycle management—the canonical manufacturing product lifecycle.

**Core Contribution:**
Prior to IOF Core's release in beta form (version 1), no mid-level manufacturing ontology grounded in BFO existed. The framework addresses manufacturing semantics, Industry 4.0 requirements, and systems integration challenges.
