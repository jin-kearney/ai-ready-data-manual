## Excerpt / Key Points

### Overall Structure
A 4×5 matrix of **capability pillars × maturity levels**.

### Four Pillars
1. **Business** — strategy actuation, business model elaboration, business enablers
2. **Organization** — leadership, product ownership, delivery, culture, capabilities
3. **Data** — data strategy, architecture, quality, governance
4. **Technology** — technology strategy, execution, user interface

### Five Maturity Levels
1. **EKG Initiation** — "organization is just starting to explore...early stages of planning or piloting"
2. **Extensible Platform** — "has implemented an EKG and is starting to build out capabilities and integrations"
3. **Enterprise Ready** — "fully implemented...established processes and infrastructure to support use across the organization"
4. **Strategic Asset** — "using EKG as a key strategic asset, leveraging capabilities to drive innovation and inform decision-making"
5. **Operational Ecosystem** — "EKG is fully integrated...key part of the organization's overall ecosystem"

### Note on Metrics
The framework does not prescribe specific numerical KPIs per level as of v1.0. It focuses on capability descriptions and dimensions. Specific metrics are expected in downstream assessment tooling.

### Relevance to Ontology Roadmap
The EKG/MM provides the best public reference for mapping ontology preparation maturity to enterprise KG adoption stages. The five levels map to ontology readiness as follows:
- Level 1 = first domain T-Box (pilot)
- Level 2 = reusable ontology components, multi-domain expansion
- Level 3 = enterprise-wide deployment, governance established
- Level 4 = ontology as strategic data asset driving decisions
- Level 5 = continuous, automated ontology evolution + agent reasoning
