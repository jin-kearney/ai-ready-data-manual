## Relevant Excerpt

Mike Uschold & Martin King (1995) "Towards a Methodology for Building Ontologies" presented at IJCAI-95 Workshop on Basic Ontological Issues in Knowledge Sharing, Montreal.

**Four Phases:**
1. **Identify purpose, scope, and scenarios for use** — Define why the ontology is needed and what questions it must answer
2. **Build the ontology** — Includes: (a) Ontology capture (identify terms, produce definitions, handle ambiguous terms); (b) Coding in formal language; (c) Integrating existing ontologies
3. **Document the ontology** — Formal documentation of all decisions
4. **Evaluate the ontology** — Test against competency questions, expert review

**Key findings from the methodology:**
- Competency questions may not be necessary before building; detailed text definitions can guide modeling instead
- Meta-ontology (upper ontology choice) can be deferred until after detailed definitions are produced
- Defining 'cognitively basic' terms first leads to less rework
- The ENTERPRISE methodology emphasizes the ontology capture phase and handling of ambiguous terms as the main obstacle to shared understanding

**Historical significance:** One of the first structured approaches; influenced all later methodologies. Based on experience building the Enterprise Ontology (covering plans, activities, resources, organization, marketing, accounting).
