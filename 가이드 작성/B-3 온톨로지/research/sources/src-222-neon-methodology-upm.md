## Relevant Excerpt

The NeOn Methodology is a scenario-based methodology supporting collaborative ontology development and reuse in distributed environments. Its key assets are nine scenarios:

1. **From Specification to Implementation** — Build from scratch; specify requirements first
2. **Reusing Non-Ontological Resources (NORs)** — Re-engineer databases, thesauri, glossaries into ontologies
3. **Reusing Ontological Resources** — Leverage existing ontologies (whole, modules, statements)
4. **Reusing and Re-engineering Ontological Resources** — Combine reuse with modification
5. **Reusing and Merging Ontological Resources** — Integrate multiple domain-specific ontologies
6. **Reusing, Merging, and Re-engineering** — Post-merge re-engineering
7. **Reusing Ontology Design Patterns (ODPs)** — Access pattern repositories
8. **Restructuring Ontological Resources** — Modularization, pruning, extension, specialization
9. **Localizing Ontological Resources** — Multilingual/cultural adaptation

**Six-Phase Life Cycle:** Initiation → Reuse → Merging → Re-engineering → Design → Implementation → Maintenance

**Key differentiator from METHONTOLOGY:** NeOn addresses ontology networks (multiple interconnected ontologies), not just single ontologies. Critical for holding company + affiliate architecture where each affiliate extends a common upper ontology.
