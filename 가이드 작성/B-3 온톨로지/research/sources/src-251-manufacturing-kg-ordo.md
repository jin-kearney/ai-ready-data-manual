## Key Excerpts

**What fails without knowledge graphs:**
"Industrial AI exposes a hard constraint: agents cannot reason across disconnected systems." Without relational structure, AI remains confined to single platforms.

**Missing navigation = broken reasoning:**
Systems showing only data flow lack "relational context," preventing both humans and AI from understanding how systems interconnect and how decisions propagate.

**Scattered knowledge problem:**
Without ontology and standardized relationships, manufacturing knowledge remains trapped in local systems, preventing enterprise-wide reasoning about anomalies and patterns.

**Concrete manufacturing use cases enabled:**

1. **Root cause analysis:** "One of the first truly practical AI use cases in manufacturing." Knowledge graphs trace from symptom through asset, work order, and historical data across disconnected systems.

2. **Production delay investigation:** AI agents traverse relationships to answer "why is a line behind schedule" by connecting production data, work orders, asset status, and constraints.

3. **Equipment contextualization:** When a PLC or asset is introduced, "the graph immediately understands where the asset sits, what it connects to, how it behaves in context."

**Systems integrated:**
ERP, MES, CRM, SCADA, IoT platforms. Knowledge graph "sits atop your data stack, intelligently weaving together what you already have."

**Decision criteria — when knowledge graph becomes necessary:**
- AI agents must answer questions spanning multiple business layers
- Root cause analysis requires cross-system investigation
- Organizations are ready with "connected, contextualized, and normalized" data

**Important prerequisite caveat:**
"Knowledge graphs expose but don't fix bad data, so foundational architecture must come first."
