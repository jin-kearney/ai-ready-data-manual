## Relevant Excerpt

**Core Definitions:**
- **Knowledge Graph**: Represents "concrete facts about specific entities and their relationships," focusing on instance-level data like "Alice works at Company X."
- **Ontology**: Defines "the types of entities, relationships, constraints, and rules that describe how knowledge in a domain is structured," operating at the conceptual level.

**Primary Differences:**

| Aspect | Knowledge Graph | Ontology |
|--------|-----------------|----------|
| Purpose | Represent real-world facts | Define concepts and meanings |
| Focus | Instance-level data | Schema and conceptual level |
| Reasoning | Optional capability | Central to design |
| Stability | Dynamic, evolving | Stable and well-defined |

**Complementary Roles:**
"Ontologies act as the semantic backbone, ensuring consistency and enabling reasoning, while the knowledge graph serves as the scalable, queryable data layer." One answers "what is happening," while the other answers "what does it mean."

**Historical Context:**
Knowledge graphs gained prominence after Google's 2012 introduction of its Knowledge Graph product, while ontologies emerged earlier through semantic web standards (RDF, OWL) in the early 2000s, rooted in AI research on knowledge representation.

**Practical Integration:**
Effective modern systems combine both: ontologies provide semantic rigor and shared vocabularies, while knowledge graphs operationalize those definitions with actual data at scale.

**Key Quote (confirmed by Neo4j):**
"A knowledge graph is the instantiation of an ontology, and an ontology is the knowledge model."
